### **Soil Nutrient Profiles for App Logic**

Here is a breakdown of the optimal, marginal (yellow), and poor (red) ranges for each crop.

**Note on N-P-K:** The values for Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), and Potassium (K) are complex as they are measured in many ways (ppm, mg/kg, or lbs/acre). For your app's "battery" logic, the simplest way is to use a qualitative ranking (Low, Medium, High). The recommendations below are based on general soil test interpretations.

---

### 1. üåæ Paddy (Rice)

Paddy is unique because it grows in flooded, anaerobic (low-oxygen) soil, which changes the chemistry. It can tolerate a wider pH range.

* **pH:**
    * **Green (Optimal):** 5.5 ‚Äì 7.0
    * **Yellow (Marginal):** 5.0 - 5.4 or 7.1 - 7.5
    * **Red (Poor):** < 5.0 or > 7.5
* **Nitrogen (N):** **High** importance. Needed for leaf and tiller growth.
* **Phosphorus (P):** **Medium** importance. Crucial for root development and early plant energy.
* **Potassium (K):** **High** importance. Needed for stem strength and grain filling.

---

### 2. ‚òÅÔ∏è Cotton

Cotton is sensitive to highly acidic soil, which can lock up nutrients.

* **pH:**
    * **Green (Optimal):** 6.0 ‚Äì 7.5
    * **Yellow (Marginal):** 5.5 - 5.9 or 7.6 - 8.0
    * **Red (Poor):** < 5.5 or > 8.0
* **Nitrogen (N):** **High** importance. Drives vegetative growth and boll production.
* **Phosphorus (P):** **Medium** importance. Essential for root growth and energy.
* **Potassium (K):** **Very High** importance. Critical for water regulation and fiber quality. Cotton is often called a "potash-hungry" crop.

---

### 3. üçÖ Tomato

Tomatoes prefer slightly acidic soil to make all nutrients available.

* **pH:**
    * **Green (Optimal):** 6.0 ‚Äì 6.8
    * **Yellow (Marginal):** 5.5 - 5.9 or 6.9 - 7.2
    * **Red (Poor):** < 5.5 or > 7.2
* **Nitrogen (N):** **High** importance. Good for initial growth, but *too much* N (a "red" high) will create all leaves and no fruit.
* **Phosphorus (P):** **High** importance. Critical for flower and fruit development.
* **Potassium (K):** **High** importance. Key for fruit quality, size, and disease resistance.

---

### 4. üå∂Ô∏è Chili

Chilis are very similar to tomatoes in their needs.

* **pH:**
    * **Green (Optimal):** 6.0 ‚Äì 7.0
    * **Yellow (Marginal):** 5.5 - 5.9 or 7.1 - 7.5
    * **Red (Poor):** < 5.5 or > 7.5
* **Nitrogen (N):** **High** importance. Like tomatoes, a good balance is needed, not too much.
* **Phosphorus (P):** **Medium** importance. For roots and flowers.
* **Potassium (K):** **High** importance. For fruit development and plant health.

---

### 5. üåΩ Maize (Corn)

Maize is a heavy "feeder," meaning it uses a lot of nutrients, especially Nitrogen.

* **pH:**
    * **Green (Optimal):** 6.0 ‚Äì 7.2
    * **Yellow (Marginal):** 5.5 - 5.9 or 7.3 - 7.8
    * **Red (Poor):** < 5.5 or > 7.8
* **Nitrogen (N):** **Very High** importance. This is the #1 nutrient for maize, driving yield.
* **Phosphorus (P):** **High** importance. Critical for early root and stalk development.
* **Potassium (K):** **High** importance. Essential for strong stalks (to prevent falling over) and water use.

---

### 6. ü´ò Pulses (Lentils, Chickpeas)

Pulses are special. They are "nitrogen-fixers," meaning they create their own Nitrogen.

* **pH:**
    * **Green (Optimal):** 5.5 ‚Äì 7.2 (Very wide range)
    * **Yellow (Marginal):** 5.0 - 5.4 or 7.3 - 8.0
    * **Red (Poor):** < 5.0 or > 8.0
* **Nitrogen (N):** **Low** importance. You only need a *small* amount of N at the start. Too much N (a "red" high) will stop the plant from fixing its own, wasting money.
* **Phosphorus (P):** **High** importance. This is the key nutrient for pulses. It fuels the nitrogen-fixation process in the root nodules.
* **Potassium (K):** **Medium** importance. Needed for overall plant health and water regulation.
